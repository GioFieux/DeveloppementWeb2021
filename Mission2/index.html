CARNIVOROUS

Raptor

T-Rex

The carnivorous dinosaurs are from the same group : the theropods. They are bipedal dinosaurs, they move using their hind legs, so that the front legs were free to perform functions other than locomotions. They have many curved and serrated teeth adapted to a carnivorous diet and have hands with curved and pointed claws.

HERBIVOROUS

Triceratops

Diplodocus

The herbivorous dinosaurs are sauropods and ornithischians. They are quadrupedal (sauropods) or can be on their hind limbs (ornithischians). They were heavy and sturdy, with weights varying from species to species (range : 0.5 tons to 123 tons). Due to their size, they were not very agile or fast. Their teeth were not very pointed but were adapted to be able to tear, crush and digest leaves.

Surface : 20 km² divided into enclosures.

Code : HER-00-20

AQUATIC

Mosasaurus

Elasmosaurus

The aquatic dinosaurs are actually reptiles and are from the Plesosauria family. They are the largest marine reptiles in the world with specimens exceeding 15 meters in length. They looked a little bit like a turtle, but more elongated and without a shell and were carnivorous animal.

Actual number of dinosaurs (max capacity of 5) :

  • 1 Mosasaurus
  • 2 Elasmosaurus

Surface : 10 km² divided into water enclosures.

Code : AQU-00-05

FLYING

Pteranodon

Quetzalcoatlus

Flying dinosaurs are actually flying reptiles and are from the familyt of pterosaurus. Their wingspan range was between 25 centimeters (Nemicolopterus) and 11 meters (Quetzalcoatlus). They had a long neck with a long skull full of needlelike teeth or teethless and had crest on tehir head. They were carnivorous animal and occasionally ate fruits.
